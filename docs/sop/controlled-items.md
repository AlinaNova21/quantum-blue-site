# Controlled Items

Unlike most other servers, in Quantum Blue you will not see contraband indicators when examining items. Instead, we have a list of definitions for contraband and controlled items for players to interpret. This is done for immersion and roleplay, as well as to give attorneys a real purpose.

Controlled items are dangerous or restricted items whose use on stations should be monitored carefully and may be authorized for use by select groups or individuals. Possession of these items without a license is a crime, with varying prison times depending on the severity and risk of the item.

Only relevant, mindshielded department heads may approve or hand out licenses to controlled items.

Note that any items found within a given job's workplace at the start of their shift, granted that an individual's authorized identification card has clearance to access that item, are by default assumed licensed to carry and use such items to do their job. Additionally, tools and equipment located within emergency lockers are not considered controlled items.

## Contraband Technology {#contraband-technology}

Below is a list of types of controlled technologies and the department heads responsible for their licensing:

- Nuclear materials, weapons, and technology (Head of Security).
- Cloaking, chameleon, and IFF-inhibiting technology (Head of Security).
- Telecommunication or signal jamming technology (Chief Engineer).
- Powered or cryptographic equipment designed for the electronic or mechanical bypass of secure-access systems (Chief Engineer).
- Hypodermic spray tools and injectors (Chief Medical Officer).
- Bluespace materials, alloy, or technology (Research Director).
- Teleportation and portal technology (Research Director).

Additionally, any equipment technology providing insight into the development, production, or procurement of a controlled item is also controlled in and of itself.

## Chemicals {#chemicals}

The Chief Medical Officer is authorized to write prescriptions as medically necessary, as well as licenses for the use of controlled substances as necessary.

### Class I - 20 minutes {#class-i}

Class I Chemicals may not be authorized, and are to be securely stored or destroyed where possible, and incur a criminal possession charge of 20 minutes.

- Romerol, or any other viral contaminants.
- Lexorin, or other rapid-acting poisons or venoms.

### Class II - 10 minutes {#class-ii}

Class II Chemicals may be licensed for specific application, incurring a criminal possession charge of 10 minutes.

- Phlogiston, and other pyrotechnic and/or explosive compounds.
- Amatoxin, and other toxic and/or poisonous compounds.
- Mindbreaker toxin and Cryptobiolin.
- Nocturine, and similar sedatives.

### Class III - 5 minutes {#class-iii}

Class III Chemicals may be prescribed for therapeutic use as found appropriate, incurring a criminal possession charge of 5 minutes.

- Desoxyephedrine and similar stimulants.
- Any psychoactives used to aid in mental well-being.

## Armaments {#armaments}

Possession may be charged for each separate and distinct weapon, with varying times depending on severity, with a time maximum of stacking possessions per category.

### Category A: 2 minutes, max 8 minutes {#category-a}

- Any object or instrument specifically designed, adapted, or used to cause physical harm in close combat.
- Any object or equipment specifically designed or adapted to protect against physical harm caused by weapons, projectiles, and other forms of direct assault.
- Any object or instrument specifically designed or adapted to incapacitate or disorient by using light, sound, or shock without causing permanent injury.
- Any object or equipment specifically designed or adapted for explicitly training purposes that would otherwise fall under another category.

### Category B: 5 minutes, max 10 minutes {#category-b}

- Any object or instrument specifically designed or adapted to cause physical harm in close combat, which employs a powered system to enhance performance, or which may slash or pierce the victim.
- Any manual action or manually loaded firearms, with either an internal magazine or no magazine at all.

### Category C: 10 minutes, max 15 minutes {#category-c}

- Any manually-operated ballistic arms that use an external magazine or feeding mechanism.
- Any semi-automatic ballistic arms that are easily concealed within a pocket or coat.
- Any object or instrument specifically designed or adapted for explicitly less-lethal or non-lethal purposes that would otherwise not fall under another category.

### Category D: 15 minutes, max 20 minutes {#category-d}

- Any semi-automatic ballistic arms that are not easily concealable.
- Any fully-automatic ballistic arms.
- Any laser, plasma, or energy-based arms, including those designed or intended for use in melee combat.
- Any object or device specifically designed or adapted to cause physical harm through detonation of an explosive charge.
- Any object or equipment specifically designed or adapted to protect against both a space environment and physical harm caused by weapons, projectiles or other forms of direct assault.

### Category E: 20 minutes. No stacking. {#category-e}

- Any emplaced weapon, such as an automated or manual turret.
- Any object or instrument specifically designed or adapted to launch large-caliber explosives or projectiles for offensive or defensive purposes.
- Any object or instrument specifically designed or adapted to employ chemical, biological, esoteric, radiological or nuclear hazards for offensive or defensive purposes.

## Licenses {#licenses}

Any individual requiring a controlled item in the course of their job or for regular function may request a document of authorization or license from the relevant departmental head. The HoS or Warden may license weapons and armor, the CMO may license chemicals, and the CE, RD, or other relevant department head may license most other items.

A license must have an itemized list of the approved controlled items, the name and position of the holder, and a signed and stamped statement by the authorizing party.

Should the holder commit crimes through the use of such authorized items, the authorizing party may be found guilty of accessory to said crimes, in addition to endangerment and abuse of power, where applicable. The Commanding Officer is also authorized to supervise the approval and revocation of documentation as they see fit.
