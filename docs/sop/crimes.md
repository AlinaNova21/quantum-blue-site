# Crimes and Sentencing Guide

## Overview

Members of station security are authorized to raise charges against suspects, given that valid probable cause is found. When a suspect is detained, they must be informed of their suspected charge and informed of what evidence the charge is based upon. Members of station security are then authorized to sentence suspects.

A detainee cannot be held awaiting sentencing for more than 5 minutes. A non-capital sentence must also not exceed 25 minutes. Further details can be found later in the document regarding details on possession charges.

If a capital offense is suspected and sentenced by security, the detainee or prisoner has the legal right to a court appeal.

Security personnel are expected to offer shorter sentences should the detainee cooperate, show signs of regret or no malice, and especially for first offenses. For the lowest severity crimes, a sentence may be skipped altogether in favor of a warning. Max sentences can often be reduced to the maximum sentence of 25 minutes, within reason.

Permanent confinement may not be offered if no personnel are able to consistently perform check-ins with the relevant prisoner, in which case the max sentence of 25 minutes should be granted instead.

Conspiring to, or knowingly facilitating a crime, earns the same sentence time as the relevant crime.

With the exception of possession charges stacking with others within the same category, stacking charges is not permitted. In case of multiple charges, only the highest severity item determines the duration of the sentence.

## Capital - Trial/Permanent Confinement {#capital}

Capital crimes require a trial and may result in permanent confinement or execution.

- **Decorporealization** - To deprive a sophont of physical form without consent.
- **Prevention of revival** - To willfully render the body of a sophont into an unrecoverable state, or to prevent medical treatment of the deceased without lawful reason.
- **Grand Sabotage** - To render a department or a large portion of the station unlivable or irreparably damaged.
- **Sedition** - To attempt to overthrow the chain of command using violence without just cause.
- **Terrorism** - To threaten violence or death while displaying probable ability and intent to enact it as a means to manipulate others into action or inaction.
- **Kidnapping** - To unlawfully restrain or deprive the free movement of a sophont.

## Grand Felony - 15 minutes {#grand-felony}

- **Black marketeering** - To purchase or sell controlled items, or mass produce them with the intent to trade.
- **Sabotage** - To render station critical infrastructure or systems inoperable.
- **Abuse of power** - To intentionally misuse or wrongfully exercise one's own authority, influence, or control resulting in harm, unjust treatment, or demonstrable loss to another entity.
- **Breach of custody** - To escape the custody of station law enforcement while serving a legal sentence.
- **Murder** - To willfully and maliciously cause the death of a sophont.

## Felony - 8 minutes {#felony}

- **Rioting** - To engage in disruptive protest and refuse to disperse.
- **Manslaughter** - To directly or indirectly cause the unintentional death of a sophont.
- **Endangerment** - To risk harm or death to another sophont through malpractice, action, or inaction where preventing it would not bring harm to oneself.
- **Grand theft** - To deprive a sophont of any controlled item or a large part of their possessions.
- **Obstruction of justice** - To wilfully disobey, interfere with, or refuse a lawful decree of law enforcement.
- **False report** - To wilfully and maliciously tell an untruth either in court or in the process of making a report to law enforcement.
- **Identity theft** - To maliciously assume the identity of a sophont, such as through writing, attire, or identification card, with the intention to mislead another sophont.

## Misdemeanor - 4 minutes {#misdemeanor}

- **Theft** - To take an item belonging to another sophont.
- **Assault** - To inflict direct physical harm upon another sophont.
- **Vandalism** - To steal or damage public station property, or property belonging to another sophont.
- **Harassment** - Repeated verbally or physically disruptive behavior directed towards an individual or group.
- **Animal cruelty** - To inflict unnecessary suffering on a sapient being with malicious intent.
- **Trespass** - To enter into an area where one is not authorized or invited.
- **Hooliganism** - To intentionally and maliciously engage in disruptive conduct in which one refuses to cease.
